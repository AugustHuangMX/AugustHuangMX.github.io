About

I am a Master's student in Econometrics and Mathematical Economics at the London School of Economics and Political Science (LSE). My research interests lie in labour economics, experimental economics, and other fields in applied microeconomics.

I am currently a remote research assistant for Professor Zihan Hu, analyzing the impacts of Mandarin proficiency on rural residents' access to government subsidies in China. I also participate in a micro theory reading group at the University of Macau, engaging with cutting-edge research in mechanism design and market design.

I completed my undergraduate studies in Economics at the University of Macau as a member of the Honors College, where I wrote my honors thesis on China's Gaokao admission mechanism reform.

📚 Check out my Digital Garden at https://hzd-digital-garden.vercel.app/ for my learning notes and research thoughts.
